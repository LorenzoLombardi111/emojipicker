Face emojis are the backbone of digital expression — the closest thing we have to conveying facial expressions through text. From beaming joy to frustrated anger, there's a smiley for every human emotion. This guide covers every face emoji in Unicode, grouped by the emotion they express, with explanations of when and how to use each one. Click any emoji to copy it instantly to your clipboard.

Face emojis are among the oldest in the Unicode standard and remain the most frequently used category. They work universally across every platform — what you send from an iPhone renders on Android, Windows, Mac, and the web. While the visual style varies slightly between platforms, the meaning stays consistent.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many smiley emojis are there?

There are over 80 smiley and face emojis in the Unicode standard, covering emotions from joy and love to anger, fear, and sickness. New face emojis are added with each Unicode release — recent additions include the melting face 🫠, saluting face 🫡, dotted line face 🫥, and shaking face 🫨. The exact count grows with each annual Unicode update.

What's the difference between 😄 and 😃?

😃 (grinning face with big eyes) has open, round eyes showing excitement or eager surprise. 😄 (grinning face with smiling eyes) has closed, squinting eyes suggesting genuine, relaxed happiness — like a real smile that reaches the eyes. 😄 feels warmer and more natural, while 😃 can feel more energetic, eager, or even slightly forced depending on context.

What does 🥲 mean?

🥲 (smiling face with tear) expresses bittersweet emotion — being happy and sad simultaneously. It can mean "this is so touching I'm emotional," "I'm grateful but hurting," or the classic "I'm fine" while clearly not fine. Added in 2020, it resonated so deeply with people that it became one of the fastest-adopted emojis ever, filling an emotional gap that no previous emoji covered.
